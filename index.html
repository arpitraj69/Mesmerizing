UYUNI SALT FLAT

Continent : South America

Country : Bolivia

About : Salar de Uyuni (or "Salar de Tunupa") is the world's largest salt flat, or playa, at 10,582 square kilometres (4,086 sq mi) in area. It is in the Daniel Campos Province in Potosí in southwest Bolivia, near the crest of the Andes at an elevation of 3,656 m (11,995 ft) above sea level.

VIKURFJARA BLACK SAND BEACH

Continent : Europe

Country : Iceland

About : Vikurfjara Black Sand Beach owes its existence to centuries of volcanic activity. The beach's distinctive black sands result from lava cooling rapidly upon meeting the ocean. This process transforms the molten lava into basalt, which is then broken down into fine sand by the relentless forces of the sea.

ZHANGJIAJIE

Continent : Asia

Country : China

About : Zhangjiajie, a city in the northwest of China's Hunan province, is home to the famed Wulingyuan Scenic Area. This protected zone encompasses thousands of jagged quartzite sandstone columns, many of which rise over 200m, as well as caves filled with stalactites and stalagmites. Wulingyuan also encompasses forests, rivers, waterfalls and 2 large natural land bridges, as well as endangered plant and animal species.

About : Lagoa Bonita is known for its crystalline blue waters, surrounded by pristine white sand dunes. These seasonal lagoons form due to rainwater accumulating between the dunes during the rainy season (typically from January to June).

Country : Argentina
